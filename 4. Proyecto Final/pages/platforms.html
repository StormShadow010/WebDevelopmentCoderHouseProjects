Steam is a video game digital distribution service and storefront from Valve. It was launched as a software client in September 2003 to provide game updates automatically for Valve's games, and expanded to distributing third-party titles in late 2005. Steam offers various features, like digital rights management (DRM), game server matchmaking with Valve Anti-Cheat measures, social networking, and game streaming services. Steam client's functions include game update automation, cloud storage for game progress, and community features such as direct messaging, in-game overlay functions and a virtual collectable marketplace.

The Epic Game Store is a new online video game store created by Epic Games, the company behind video games like 'Fortnite'. And it is precisely the good reputation that Epic has managed to achieve thanks to this game and the development of the popular Unreal Engine that the store positions itself as a rival to take into account for the veteran Steam, since its operation is similar to that of this and other online game stores. During the last five years, this company has tried to carve out a way to offer its own products directly to users, without the need to go through other online stores or intermediaries.This has made them take risky decisions.

Origin is the free application of Electronic Arts' digital distribution platform for PC and mobile devices. Through it you can buy and play FIFA 23 or any other game in its catalog. To give you an idea, Origin works in a similar way to Steam. However, it has a better streamlined design and gives exclusive access to EA titles.

In short, if you like to play EA games on your computer or on your mobile phone, Origin is an essential application. Through it you will have the possibility of acquiring new titles and managing your collection with total freedom. Test it!
